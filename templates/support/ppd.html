Understanding Postpartum Depression

Postpartum depression (PPD) affects many new mothers. It can cause feelings of sadness, guilt, and even hopelessness after childbirth. We’re here to support you through this difficult time.

What is PPD?

Postpartum depression is a common condition that affects new mothers, typically starting within the first year after childbirth. It can affect your emotional and physical health.

Symptoms of PPD:

  • Feeling sad, empty, or hopeless
  • Difficulty bonding with your baby
  • Changes in sleep or appetite
  • Thoughts of harming yourself or your baby

How to Seek Help:

If you think you may have PPD, talk to your doctor or a mental health professional. You don’t have to go through this alone.
